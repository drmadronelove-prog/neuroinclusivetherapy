Spark & Don't Make It Worse

When distress arrives, the first rule isn't fix it — it's don't add fuel. Practice recognizing which responses escalate activation, and which ones just let the spark settle.

How it works

A situation sparks distress. For each possible response, decide whether it makes it worse or doesn't make it worse. You'll get feedback on why. The goal isn't to feel better — it's to stop making things harder than they need to be.

The core idea: "Don't make it worse" is a DBT distress tolerance skill. In crisis, you don't need to solve anything. You only need to not escalate. That's the whole skill.

The rules of don't-make-it-worse

  • ↓ You don't have to fix the feeling. You only have to not escalate it.
  • ↓ Acting from a hot nervous system usually adds fuel. Pause first.
  • ↓ Reassurance-seeking resets the anxiety loop higher, not lower.
  • ↓ Moving your body works — not because it fixes things, but because it shifts your state.
  • ↓ Naming what's happening ("I'm in distress") creates a gap between you and it.
